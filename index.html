BTS, or Bangtan Sonyeondan (Beyond The Scene), is a Grammy-nominated South Korean boy band that debuted in June 2013. They have gained global fame for their self-produced music and record-breaking achievements. Known as "21st Century Pop Icons", they have earned four No. 1 songs, sold-out stadiums, and been named TIME's Entertainer of the Year 2020. Their dedicated fan base, ARMY (Adorable Representative M.C. for Youth), is known for spreading love and positivity worldwide.

Demographics of ARMY

After discovering BTS's massive popularity, you are probably wondering who makes up their dedicated fanbase, ARMY.

Well, ARMY spans across the globe, making it hard to sum them up in just a few words. However, we can give you some insights into their demographics.

Thus, a survey was conducted in 2022 to gather data on the fanbase and better understand who ARMY is.

ARMY Age Distribution

18-29 age group is the largest within the ARMY community, accounting for 53.63% (301,566 fans).

This global community is incredibly diverse, spanning various age groups and genders. While ARMY includes fans of all ages, a significant portion falls within the 18-29 age range, with many younger fans (teens and young adults) actively engaging in fandom activities.

Gender-wise, the ARMY community is predominantly female, accounting for 96.23% of total survey respondents. Despite the large female majority, the fanbase is seeing a growing number of male fans, as well as fans of other genders, including non-binary individuals, within the ARMY community.

This diversity highlights BTS's broad appeal and their unique ability to connect with people from different backgrounds through their meaningful music and positive messages.

ARMY Evolution Across Various Platforms (January 3, 2020 - August 30, 2020)

Although all members of BTS served in the military in 2023, the fanbase reached its highest peak compared to other years.

Over the years, the ARMY community has expanded across various platforms, not only limited to social media but also music platforms. In 2023, the number of fans peaked at approximately 14.84 billion followers in total, even though the group was on hiatus.

Platforms like Instagram and YouTube have gained significant traction, with YouTube followers reaching approximately 4 billion and Instagram followers reaching approximately 3.9 billion in 2023.

This highlights a shift in how fans interact with BTS. This evolution reflects the band's expanding influence and the increasing diversity of their audience, as fans embrace multiple channels to connect with their favorite artists.
